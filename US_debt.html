Introduction

As an Analytics onsultant, I was hired by a debt agency in Washington DC to analyze the US government's public and governmental debt to answer some questions.

Business Questions

  1. What was the yearly debt percentage increase for each year compared to the previous year?
  2. Which months historically have seen the highest/lowest increases in total debt?
  3. What was the projected growth of the publicly held debt in the next few years?

About the data

Bike rentals during the year of 2019 and 2020 were combined into one. Data consists of 1 table consisting of 14 fields and 288443 records in csv format.

Tools Used

Excel (Data Cleaning)

Insights

    1. Yearly Debt

    For yearly Debt, data between the years of 2016 and 2022 was analyzed

  • For the years 2016-2019, growth was relatively moderate, about 6-9%.
  • During the pandemic (year 2020) public debt spiked drmatically by ~26%
  • Growth rates sharply declined to about 5-6 after the pandemic
    • 2. Monthly Average

    For monthly average, data between the years of 1997 and 2007 was analyzed

  • For the years 2016-2019, growth was relatively moderate, about 6-9%.
  • During the pandemic (year 2020) public debt spiked drmatically by ~26%
  • Growth rates sharply declined to about 5-6%.
      • 3. Projected Growth

      Publicly projected debt data between the years of 1997 and 2027 was analyzed

    • Highest debt increase is seen in January and December.
    • Lowest debt increase is seen in the summer months.
    • Debt continues to climb steeply.
